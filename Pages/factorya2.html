Packaging:

After passing quality control, the ground coffee is ready for packaging. Coffee packaging involves several considerations, including packaging material, size, design, and labeling. Different packaging formats, such as bags, cans, or pods, may be used depending on consumer preferences and market trends.

Sealing and Labeling:

Once the ground coffee is placed in the packaging, it is sealed to preserve freshness and prevent oxidation. Sealing methods vary but often include heat sealing or vacuum sealing. Additionally, each package is labeled with essential information such as the coffee blend, roast level, expiration date, and origin.
